# Hackin 2026 - Experiences Launchpad

## One-line Pitch

**Experiences Launchpad lets physical, travel-adjacent partners launch a Headout-powered experiences business in minutes: branded QR storefront, AI concierge, curated inventory, checkout, fulfillment, QR-attributed commission, and rev-share dashboard.**

Use **HO for Hotels** as the easiest stage demo if needed, but pitch **Experiences Launchpad** as a multi-partner platform for hotels, restaurants, airport lounges, event venues, conference hotels, theatres, cruise terminals, and tourist desks.

---

## Core Idea

Headout already has the hardest parts of selling experiences:
- global inventory
- availability
- pricing
- checkout
- payment rails
- ticket fulfillment
- support
- cancellation/refund operations
- supplier relationships

But millions of high-intent travel moments happen outside Headout, especially in physical places where people are already asking "what next?":
- hotel front desks
- restaurant tables and bill folders
- airport and airline lounges
- conference hotels
- event venues
- theatre/sports/concert venues
- cruise terminals and ferry lounges
- coworking communities
- destination wedding pages
- tourist information desks

These partners have trust, distribution, footfall, or audience. They do not have inventory, fulfillment, support, or payments.

**Experiences Launchpad gives them a zero-engineering way to sell Headout experiences under their own physical context, while Headout becomes the experience infrastructure layer behind the places where travelers already ask for plans.**

Every partner QR is also an attribution surface. When a guest books after scanning a restaurant, hotel, lounge, or event-venue QR, that partner gets a cut of the booking. This makes the QR valuable enough to place in high-traffic physical surfaces, not just a passive marketing link.

---

## Partnerships Reality Check

`#pod-partnerships` shows that Headout already has serious machinery around the existing digital distribution lanes:
- creator and affiliate tooling
- affiliate booking APIs
- partner portal and signup revamp
- voucher APIs
- product webhooks for partners
- Prio/agent/API attribution
- whitelabel work
- reporting and dashboard asks from existing affiliates and API partners

That means Launchpad should **not** be pitched as "creators can build storefronts" or "travel agents get a curated page." Those are too close to active distribution, affiliate, and API partner work.

The better wedge is:

> **Physical-context partners who have guest intent but no experiences infrastructure.**

Hotels are one clean demo, not the boundary. The same system should expand to restaurants, airport lounges, event venues, conference hotels, and other places where guests are physically present, time-constrained, and ready to decide.

## Physical Partner Wedges

### Why Physical Partners First

Hotels are the cleanest demo, but the idea should stay multi-partner from day one. The common pattern across hotels, restaurants, lounges, and event venues is:

- the guest is already physically present
- the guest has time to fill or a next-plan question
- the partner has trust but no experience inventory
- the partner can place QR surfaces where intent appears
- Headout can fulfill the booking and share revenue

Every hotel front desk answers the same question all day:

> "What should we do today?"

Today that answer is usually:
- a tired brochure rack
- a front-desk recommendation
- a Google Maps search
- a concierge phone call
- a WhatsApp message to a local operator

None of that is scalable, multilingual, measurable, or directly monetized.

With Launchpad, each partner gets:
- QR codes for its physical surfaces: lobby, table, receipt, lounge, room, venue screen, WiFi page, or pre-arrival email
- a branded AI concierge
- curated nearby Headout inventory
- weather/time/location-aware recommendations
- in-language chat
- checkout on Headout rails
- rev-share dashboard

The partner earns a cut from bookings made through its QR. The guest gets a better answer. Headout gets a physical brand moment and a low-CAC booking channel.

### Example Demo: Hotel First

Set up a fake partner:

**Partner:** Casa Aurelia Rome Hotel  
**Location:** Near Vatican City  
**Guest mix:** Japanese, English, Hindi  
**Brand tone:** calm, premium, family-friendly  
**Commission:** 8 percent  
**Inventory:** Rome experiences around Vatican, Colosseum, food tours, museums  

Demo flow:
1. Hotel manager signs up in Launchpad.
2. They enter hotel name, address, guest profile, preferred categories, and brand style.
3. Launchpad generates:
   - branded mobile storefront
   - lobby QR code
   - AI concierge
   - curated Rome inventory
   - partner dashboard
4. Guest scans the QR from the lobby.
5. Guest asks: "It is raining and I have a 6-year-old. What can we do this afternoon?"
6. Concierge recommends 2-3 real-feeling options that fit:
   - indoor
   - nearby
   - available today
   - family-friendly
   - bookable now
7. Guest books one.
8. Hotel dashboard updates: booking value, commission earned, conversion rate.

Stage line:

> "The hotel used to answer this question for free. Now the same question becomes a booking."

Then reveal that the same flow can be switched to a restaurant table QR, an airport lounge delay QR, or an event-venue post-show QR.

## Business Model

### Revenue

Headout earns normal booking margin.

Partner earns commission:
- hotel: 8-10 percent
- restaurant/bar: 5-8 percent
- airport/lounge operator: negotiated rev share
- conference/event: sponsor or commission model

Attribution:
- every QR is tied to a partner, surface, and location
- bookings created after a scan are attributed to that partner
- partner dashboard shows scans, chats, bookings, GMV, conversion, and commission
- Headout can compare physical surfaces by revenue per QR placement

### Why Partners Say Yes

Hotels:
- monetize concierge questions
- improve guest experience
- no fulfillment burden
- multilingual service without staffing

Restaurants and bars:
- monetize the "what next?" moment after a meal
- give tourists a better local recommendation
- turn table tents, bill folders, and receipts into booking surfaces

Airport and airline lounges:
- monetize layovers, delays, and early arrivals
- improve passenger experience
- offer time-boxed plans that account for flight departure buffers
- ancillary revenue

Event venues:
- keep guests engaged before and after the main event
- create a city itinerary around shows, games, and concerts
- earn commission without operating tours

### Why Headout Says Yes

- lower CAC
- contextual acquisition at the moment of physical intent
- partner-led demand outside existing affiliate/API lanes
- new B2B2C channel
- defensible distribution
- more data about high-intent travel contexts
- offline brand presence in trusted local environments
- measurable brand placements, not vague awareness campaigns

## Risks and Answers

### "Isn't this just affiliate links?"

No. Affiliate links are static links. Launchpad gives partners a branded concierge, curated inventory, checkout handoff, fulfillment, support, and rev-share reporting.

### "Why would physical partners use this instead of Viator/GetYourGuide?"

They might use whoever makes it easiest. The wedge is not just inventory; it is a five-minute launch, multilingual concierge, local context, partner-branded QR, QR-attributed commission, and dashboard. Headout can win by being lighter and more partner-native.

### "Will this cannibalize Headout direct traffic?"

Hotel lobby scans, restaurant table QR scans, lounge delay flows, and event-venue pages are incremental contexts. These are moments where users are not necessarily opening Headout first, and they are not the same as existing creator/affiliate/API partner traffic.

### "Can this be built in 42 hours?"

Yes, if the build is one city, one partner, mocked checkout, and a polished flow. The prototype needs to prove the loop, not integrate every real system.

### "Is the AI necessary?"

The AI is useful because the partner context is messy: location, language, weather, time, guest constraints, and inventory fit. But the AI should not do math. Use deterministic filters for distance, availability, category, and time; use AI for conversation and explanation.

### "What is the moat?"

The moat is not the generated page. The moat is Headout's inventory, fulfillment, supplier operations, payment rails, support, partner distribution data, and offline brand presence. Over time, Launchpad learns which physical contexts convert which experiences.
