#Here by Headout — PARTNER Launch Trailer Prompts (Distribution side)

Companion to the guest-side trailer (flow-trailer-prompts.md). Different audience, different promise. This one sells the place owner — the GM, café/hostel owner, lounge operator, venue lead, visitor-desk manager (§15 GTM). The pitch is revenue + zero effort + a better guest experience, not guest delight.

The partner one-liner: *You already have the footfall and the trust. Add

Headout's inventory, checkout, and fulfillment with one QR — and earn a cut of

every booking.*

Use with Google Flow (Veo 3). Attach the same Headout city landing screenshots as Ingredients / style references (palette, typography, purple). UI shots (Scenes 3, 4, 6) are best as Screen Studio captures of the real build — Flow prompts are fallbacks.

#Pacing arc — confident open → fast proof → strong close

Partner trailers sell trust, not tension. Open confident and aspirational (not "lost"), accelerate through the proof (setup → live → earnings), peak on the "any place" montage, land on a clear call to action.

#Scene 1 — The missed moment (owner's POV) · ~4s · CONFIDENT OPEN

"what should we do today?"; the friendly owner/staffer wants to help but can

only shrug or wave vaguely — the guest drifts off, the moment (and the

booking) gone. Frame it warmly, not as failure: this is opportunity, not

embarrassment.

#Music & sound design — built to the pacing arc

Same build philosophy as the guest trailer, slightly more confident / corporate-uplifting (this is a business pitch, not an emotional story).

Track brief (Suno / Udio):

*"35–40s confident, uplifting corporate brand-film score. Starts warm and

understated — light piano/keys with a steady pulse. Builds with claps and

rising synths into a driving, optimistic, energetic peak with a strong beat,

then a clean resonant hit and release on the final note. Premium, modern,

trustworthy, motivating. No vocals."*

#Hit points

  • Scene 3 — "Generate" tap: the button press lands on the first downbeat

of the build (the music "turns on" with the product).

  • Scene 6 — commission ticks up: a small, satisfying accent chime on the

earnings beat — this is the emotional/financial high of the pitch.

  • Scene 7 — montage: every owner/place cut snaps on the kick.
  • Scene 8 — pull-back: biggest hit fires on the release; banner + CTA animate

in on the final sustained note.

Tools: Suno/Udio for the custom track (confirm usage rights) or Epidemic/ Artlist (search corporate uplifting build / confident montage) · ElevenLabs VO mixed ~6 dB above music · CapCut for beat-sync + auto-ducking.

#How the two trailers relate

  • Guest trailer (flow-trailer-prompts.md): emotional, story-driven —

proves the experience (Five Stars). Audience: travelers / judges feeling the product.

  • Partner trailer (this file): confident, ROI-driven — proves the channel

(Move the Needle). Audience: place owners / judges evaluating the business.

  • Shared assets: same Headout-purple QR, same "Here by Headout" lockup, same

Rome world, same screenshot references → the two films feel like one brand. Reuse Scene 7's montage footage across both; only the labels/VO framing differ.
