# Headout Here — Google Flow Trailer Prompts (All Scenes)

Use with **Google Flow (Veo 3)**. Attach your **Headout city landing-page
screenshots** as Ingredients / style references — Flow derives the palette,
typography, and photography style from them. Each scene below is a complete,
self-contained prompt (brand style already baked in) — just copy, attach the
screenshots, generate.

**Attach in Flow:** *Ingredients to Video* → add 2–3 Headout screenshots (one
with header/logo + purple, one with experience cards, one with typography/
layout) + optionally your real `<BrandedQRPoster>` export.

---

## Pacing arc — slow open → very fast build

The trailer **starts slow and deliberate, then accelerates hard** into a
rapid-fire peak, before landing on a held final beat. Drive this two ways:
**(a) clip length in the CapCut edit**, and **(b) camera-motion speed in the
Flow prompt itself.**

| Phase | Scenes | Feel | Cut length | Camera motion |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Slow open** | 1–2 | Calm, cinematic, lets the problem land | 3–5s holds | slow push-in |
| **Quickening** | 3–6 | Momentum builds, beat picks up | 1.5–2.5s | brisker moves, snap transitions |
| **Fast peak** | 7 | Rapid-fire, energy maxes out | 0.4–0.8s per location | hard snap cuts on the beat |
| **Land** | 8 + banner | Sudden release, hold on the logo | 4–5s | slow pull-back, then freeze |

- **Sync to music:** drop the cuts onto the beat (CapCut beat-detect). The
  music should build with the visuals — sparse intro → driving build → a hit on
  Scene 7 → resolve on the banner.
- **The accelerando is the point:** Scenes 1→7 should feel like the pace keeps
  doubling, then Scene 8 hits the brakes for the "Here by Headout" payoff.

> **Scenes 4–6 are UI shots.** Flow/Veo will invent fake, off-brand interfaces.
> Strongly prefer **Screen Studio captures of the real build** for these. Flow
> prompts are included below only as a fallback if you can't capture live.

## Scene 1 — The hook (lost family at the desk) · ~5s · SLOW OPEN

> **Beat / depth:** establish the universal "what do we do now?" moment and the
> three characters we follow the whole film — two parents and a ~6-year-old
> girl, fresh off arrival with a small suitcase. Plant the tension early: the
> daughter is restless, the parents are unsure, and **rain has just started
> outside** — the day's plan is suddenly in question. This quiet problem is what
> every later beat pays off. Hold it slow; let the uncertainty land.

```
A young international family — two parents and their restless six-year-old
daughter, a small wheeled suitcase beside them — stands at an elegant boutique
hotel front desk in Rome, just arrived. The parents glance at each other,
slightly unsure, one half-shrugging as if to say "so... what do we do now?";
the little girl tugs at her mother's sleeve, bored and impatient. Through the
glass entrance behind them, a sunlit Roman street has just turned to soft rain,
droplets streaking the window — the day's plan suddenly in question. Warm lobby
light, marble and brass details, dust motes in a shaft of light. Camera slowly
pushes in on the parents' uncertain faces. Candid, documentary-cinematic,
quietly hopeful mood. Premium modern travel-tech brand film, matching the color
palette, typography feel, and bright editorial photography style of the
attached Headout screenshots — especially the signature Headout purple accent.
Cinematic, shallow depth of field, soft natural light, smooth slow camera
motion, high production value, 4K, photoreal. No text overlays, no captions, no
watermarks, no invented logos.
```

## Music & sound design — built to the pacing arc

One track that **starts sparse and intimate, builds steadily, hits hard at the
montage, then releases** to land the banner. The music *is* the pacing engine —
cut the picture to its beat, not the other way around.

**Track brief (paste into Suno / Udio):**
> *"30–40s cinematic brand-film score. Starts soft and intimate — a single warm
> piano motif with light ambient texture. Gentle pulse enters and builds with
> claps/percussion and rising synths through the middle. Drops into a driving,
> energetic, uplifting electronic-orchestral peak with a strong four-on-the-floor
> beat, then a sudden hit and a clean, resonant release on the final note.
> Modern, premium, hopeful, travel-tech. No vocals."*

### Cue sheet

| Phase | Scenes | Music | Energy |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Slow open** | 1–2 | Solo piano motif + soft room ambience. Sparse, breathing, almost silent under the dialogue beat. | ▁▁ |
| **Quickening** | 3–6 | Pulse/heartbeat kick enters on the Scene 3 scan-bloom; percussion + rising synths layer in with each cut; tempo *feels* like it's accelerating. | ▃▄▅ |
| **Fast peak** | 7 | Full drop — driving beat, all elements in, montage cuts land **on every kick**. Loudest, busiest moment. | ███ |
| **Land** | 8 + banner | One big hit as the camera pulls back → music thins to a single sustained pad/piano chord → resonant tail under the "Here by Headout" banner. | █▁ |

### Hit points (sync these exactly)

- **Scene 3 — scan bloom:** the purple light-wipe lands **on the first downbeat**
  of the build. This is the moment the music "turns on."
- **Scene 5 — booking confirm:** a small bell/UI chime or accent stab on the
  purple checkmark.
- **Scene 7 — montage:** every location cut snaps **on the kick** (use CapCut
  beat-detect; aim ~0.4–0.8s per shot so cuts ride the fastest beat).
- **Scene 8 — pull-back:** the **biggest hit of the track** fires as the camera
  releases, then everything drops away to the tail.
- **Banner:** logo animates in on the final sustained note; let it ring ~1–1.5s.

### Sound-design accents (subtle, optional)

Rain ambience under Scenes 1–3 (ties the rainy-day thread) · a soft *whoosh* on
the Scene 3 light-wipe · gentle UI ticks as the dashboard numbers climb in
Scene 6 · room tone drops out for a half-beat just before the Scene 7 drop (the
classic "breath before the peak").

**Tools:** **Suno / Udio** for the custom track (fastest, fits the brief
exactly — confirm usage rights for submission) · or **Epidemic Sound / Artlist**
for a licensed "build" track (search *cinematic build / corporate uplifting
montage*) · **ElevenLabs** for VO, mixed ~6 dB above the music · final beat-sync
and ducking in **CapCut** (auto-ducks music under VO).

## Flow / Veo tips

- **Generate Friday night** — expect 3–5 takes per scene; you only need the best
  ~3–6s from each.
- Keep each request **single-action**; Veo holds one clear motion best.
- If a clip drifts off-brand, **re-attach the screenshots** and add *"strictly
  match the Headout purple of the references."*
- Capture scenes **4–6 from the real build** if at all possible — fake UI is the
  fastest way to look unpolished to judges.
