# Headout Here - GTM Plan

## Positioning

**Headout Here turns trusted physical places into Headout-powered experience storefronts.**

Hotels, restaurants, airport lounges, event venues, conference hotels, tourist desks, and other high-footfall partners get a branded QR concierge. Guests scan, ask what to do next, book on Headout rails, and the partner earns a cut from every QR-attributed booking.

This is both:
- **Distribution:** low-CAC bookings from physical surfaces where travelers already have intent.
- **Brand positioning:** Headout becomes the helpful answer to "what should we do next?" in the real world, not only an app or website users remember to open.

---

## GTM Thesis

Headout should not launch Headout Here as a generic partner portal.

Launch it as a **city-by-city physical network**:

> "In Rome, every trusted hotel lobby, restaurant table, lounge, and venue can become a Headout concierge."

The first goal is not to onboard every partner category. The first goal is to prove that physical QR surfaces can produce incremental, attributable bookings and measurable brand presence.

### Commission Model

Keep it simple for pilots:

| Partner Type | Suggested Cut | Notes |
|---|---:|---|
| Hotels | 8-10% | Strongest trust + repeated guest flow |
| Restaurants/bars | 5-8% | Higher scan volume, lower intent quality |
| Airport/lounges | Negotiated | Often commercial partnership-driven |
| Event venues | Rev-share or sponsorship | May prefer fixed sponsorship plus upside |
| Tourist desks | 5-10% | Depends on staffing and placement |

Attribution rule:
- scan creates a partner/session attribution token
- booking within attribution window credits the partner
- dashboard breaks down performance by QR surface
- partner earns commission only on completed bookings

Suggested attribution window:
- same-session by default
- 7 days for hotel pre-arrival or room QR
- same-day for restaurants, lounges, tourist desks, and venues

## Launch Motion

## Phase 0: Internal Validation

Timeline: 1-2 weeks

Goal:
- prove the concept with mock or limited real inventory before external partner promises.

Actions:
- pick Rome as pilot city
- curate 30-50 experiences across 5 use cases:
  - rainy afternoon
  - family-friendly
  - after dinner
  - 3-hour window
  - near Vatican / Colosseum
- build partner QR variants:
  - hotel lobby
  - restaurant table
  - lounge delay
  - event pre/post
- define attribution and commission logic
- create a one-page partner sales sheet

Success criteria:
- full guest flow works end to end
- dashboard shows surface-level attribution
- concierge never recommends infeasible experiences

## Phase 1: Concierge Hotel Pilot

Timeline: 4-6 weeks

Goal:
- prove physical QR surfaces produce attributable bookings in one city.

Target:
- 10-20 hotels in Rome
- prioritize boutique hotels near Vatican, Colosseum, and Centro Storico

Acquisition:
- use existing BD/partnership relationships where possible
- direct outreach to hotel GMs
- offer zero setup fee and founding partner badge
- pitch as guest experience + new revenue

Pilot promise:
- no integration
- no inventory risk
- no support burden
- QR assets delivered by Headout
- monthly commission payout

Success metrics:
- 10+ active partners
- 500+ scans/month
- 20%+ scan-to-chat rate
- 5%+ scan-to-booking rate as an early target
- positive partner feedback
- at least one partner asks for more QR surfaces

## Phase 2: Surface Expansion

Timeline: 6-10 weeks after hotel pilot

Goal:
- prove Headout Here is not hotel-only.

Add:
- 5 tourist-heavy restaurants
- 1-2 airport/lounge pilots if accessible
- 1 event venue or theatre partner
- 1 tourist desk / visitor center

Experiment:
- compare conversion by physical context
- compare QR placement quality
- compare partner category CAC
- test messaging variants:
  - "Things to do nearby"
  - "What should we do next?"
  - "Rainy day plans"
  - "Tonight near you"

Success metrics:
- booking conversion by surface
- commission earned per partner
- GMV per QR placement
- repeat scans per partner
- guest NPS / thumbs-up feedback

## Operational Requirements

### Needed From Headout

- curated city inventory
- QR attribution
- partner dashboard
- payout process
- support routing
- content moderation / recommendation safety
- partner onboarding owner
- city launch owner

### Needed From Partner

- permission to place QR assets
- basic brand/logo if desired
- preferred guest language and tone
- surface placement commitment
- payout details

### Recommendation Quality Rules

The concierge should never recommend purely by semantic similarity. It must check:
- distance
- travel time
- opening hours
- last entry
- available slots
- weather fit
- duration vs guest time window
- kid/family/accessibility constraints where provided

## Risks and Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| QR scans are low | Give partners multiple surfaces, test CTAs, show missed revenue |
| Partners forget to place QR | Provide physical collateral and first-week activation check |
| Guests ask unsupported questions | Keep concierge grounded in inventory and fallback to safe city advice |
| Recommendations are infeasible | Deterministic checks before AI wording |
| Partner payout disputes | Transparent attribution window and dashboard |
| Looks like affiliate links | Lead with concierge, local context, fulfillment, and support |
| Brand gets hidden behind partner | Use "Powered by Headout Here" visibly on every surface |
| Too many partner types dilute GTM | Launch city-by-city, start with hotels, expand only after proof |
