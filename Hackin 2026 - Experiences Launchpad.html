Experiences Launchpad

A physical partner platform that lets hotels, restaurants, airport lounges, event venues, and other trusted local surfaces launch a Headout-powered experiences business in minutes.

Partner surfaces Hotel lobby, restaurant table, airport lounge, event venue, tourist desk: QR -> concierge -> booking.
Partner cut Every booking made through a partner QR is attributed back, and the partner earns a cut.
Brand play Headout becomes the helpful answer to "what should we do next?" in the real world.

Thesis

Headout already has the hard parts of selling experiences: global inventory, availability, pricing, checkout, payments, ticket fulfillment, support, cancellations, refunds, and supplier relationships.

What Headout does not fully own is the moment when a traveler is physically present somewhere and asks: "What should we do next?"

Experiences Launchpad turns that physical intent into a Headout booking, using a partner-branded QR storefront, AI concierge, QR attribution, and partner commission.

Every partner QR is an attribution surface. When a guest books after scanning a hotel, restaurant, lounge, event-venue, or tourist-desk QR, that partner gets a cut of the booking.

Launchpad whitespace

  • Time-constrained decisions
  • No existing experiences infrastructure

Do not pitch this as "creators get storefronts" or "travel agents get curated pages." Pitch it as physical-context partners who have guest intent but no experiences infrastructure.

Sharp pitch

"Headout has inventory, fulfillment, payments, and support. Hotels, restaurants, airport lounges, and event venues have guests physically asking what to do next. Launchpad connects the two: partners earn from QR bookings, and Headout becomes the helpful experiences brand in the real world."

Business Model

Partner commission

  • Hotel: 8-10 percent
  • Restaurant/bar: 5-8 percent
  • Airport/lounge: negotiated rev share
  • Conference/event: rev share or sponsor model

Each QR is tied to a partner, surface, and location. Bookings after scan are attributed back to that distributor.

Why Headout says yes

  • Lower CAC
  • Contextual acquisition at physical intent
  • Partner-led demand outside affiliate/API lanes
  • Defensible B2B2C channel
  • Data on high-intent travel contexts
  • Offline brand presence in trusted local environments

Risks and Answers

Is this just affiliate links?

No. Affiliate links are static. Launchpad gives partners a branded concierge, curated inventory, checkout handoff, fulfillment, support, QR attribution, commission, and rev-share reporting.

Why physical partners instead of Viator or GetYourGuide?

The wedge is a five-minute launch, multilingual concierge, local context, partner-branded QR, QR-attributed commission, and dashboard. Headout can win by being lighter and more partner-native.

Will this cannibalize direct traffic?

Hotel lobby scans, restaurant table scans, lounge delays, and venue pages are incremental contexts where users may not open Headout first.

Why is this a brand play?

Every QR is a measurable offline brand placement. Headout shows up as the useful concierge exactly when travelers ask what to do next.
