# Here by Headout — PARTNER Launch Trailer Prompts (Distribution side)

Companion to the guest-side trailer (`flow-trailer-prompts.md`). **Different
audience, different promise.** This one sells *the place owner* — the GM,
café/hostel owner, lounge operator, venue lead, visitor-desk manager (§15 GTM).
The pitch is **revenue + zero effort + a better guest experience**, not guest
delight.

> **The partner one-liner:** *You already have the footfall and the trust. Add
> Headout's inventory, checkout, and fulfillment with one QR — and earn a cut of
> every booking.*

Use with **Google Flow (Veo 3)**. Attach the same **Headout city landing
screenshots** as Ingredients / style references (palette, typography, purple).
UI shots (Scenes 3, 4, 6) are best as **Screen Studio captures of the real
build** — Flow prompts are fallbacks.

---

## Pacing arc — confident open → fast proof → strong close

Partner trailers sell trust, not tension. Open **confident and aspirational**
(not "lost"), accelerate through the *proof* (setup → live → earnings), peak on
the "any place" montage, land on a clear **call to action**.

| Phase | Scenes | Feel | Cut length | Camera |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Confident open** | 1–2 | Busy, thriving place; reframe the missed moment as opportunity | 3–4s | smooth, observational |
| **The proof** | 3–6 | Setup → QR → guests book → you earn. Momentum builds. | 1.5–2.5s | brisk, snappy |
| **Fast peak** | 7 | Every kind of place, all earning | 0.5–0.8s | hard snap cuts on the beat |
| **Land + CTA** | 8 + banner | Release, scale, "become a partner" | 4–5s | slow pull-back, freeze |

Music: same build philosophy as the guest trailer — sparse → driving → hit →
release. See the cue sheet at the bottom.

## Music & sound design — built to the pacing arc

Same build philosophy as the guest trailer, slightly more **confident /
corporate-uplifting** (this is a business pitch, not an emotional story).

**Track brief (Suno / Udio):**
> *"35–40s confident, uplifting corporate brand-film score. Starts warm and
> understated — light piano/keys with a steady pulse. Builds with claps and
> rising synths into a driving, optimistic, energetic peak with a strong beat,
> then a clean resonant hit and release on the final note. Premium, modern,
> trustworthy, motivating. No vocals."*

### Cue sheet

| Phase | Scenes | Music | Energy |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Confident open** | 1–2 | Warm keys + light pulse; understated, room to breathe under VO. | ▁▃ |
| **The proof** | 3–6 | Pulse/kick enters on the Scene 3 "Generate" tap; layers build with each proof beat; tempo feels accelerating. | ▃▄▅ |
| **Fast peak** | 7 | Full drop — driving beat, montage cuts land on every kick. | ███ |
| **Land + CTA** | 8 + banner | Big hit on the pull-back → thin to a sustained pad → resonant tail under the CTA banner. | █▁ |

### Hit points

- **Scene 3 — "Generate" tap:** the button press lands on the **first downbeat**
  of the build (the music "turns on" with the product).
- **Scene 6 — commission ticks up:** a small, satisfying accent chime on the
  earnings beat — this is the emotional/financial high of the pitch.
- **Scene 7 — montage:** every owner/place cut snaps **on the kick**.
- **Scene 8 — pull-back:** biggest hit fires on the release; banner + CTA animate
  in on the final sustained note.

**Tools:** Suno/Udio for the custom track (confirm usage rights) or Epidemic/
Artlist (search *corporate uplifting build / confident montage*) · ElevenLabs
VO mixed ~6 dB above music · CapCut for beat-sync + auto-ducking.

## How the two trailers relate

- **Guest trailer** (`flow-trailer-prompts.md`): emotional, story-driven —
  proves the *experience* (Five Stars). Audience: travelers / judges feeling the
  product.
- **Partner trailer** (this file): confident, ROI-driven — proves the *channel*
  (Move the Needle). Audience: place owners / judges evaluating the business.
- **Shared assets:** same Headout-purple QR, same "Here by Headout" lockup, same
  Rome world, same screenshot references → the two films feel like one brand.
  Reuse Scene 7's montage footage across both; only the labels/VO framing differ.
